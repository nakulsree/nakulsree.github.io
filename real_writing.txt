Honestly, ever since I started buying second-hand clothes, normal clothing stores just aren’t doing it for me anymore. Every time I go shopping now, it’s impossible for me to find anything worth buying. It’s all just the same basic pieces and whatever is trending right now. No fun patterns, interesting details, or even colours anymore. I swear some tops look like they were designed by someone whose only inspiration was a beige kitchen towel (and the quality isn’t any better either)

 Maybe that’s just me, but fashion feels so much less personal now. Whenever I go thrifting, I find so many interesting pieces that actually feel unique (okay, maybe not those random printed shirts but wtv) while in modern stores everything is just a different version of the same outfit.


Also, because of that, a lot of people just look the same. I do feel like individuality is slowly coming back now – but still not for the risk of going against the expectations of others too much. For me, fashion is a way to express myself – so I think it’s kind of sad to see everyone just dressing the same in fear of being too different. Then again: I also think if big brands would offer a bigger variety of clothes and styles and stop producing solely for micro-trends, maybe people would actually buy pieces that resonate with them. Like, it’s always so cool when I see people who aren’t afraid to experiment with their style and dress regardless of what others might think.

That’s why I really wish for bigger labels to start producing more colourful and detailed clothes again. Ngl I’m tired of only seeing the same five shades in a crowd of people when fashion could be so much more fun.
